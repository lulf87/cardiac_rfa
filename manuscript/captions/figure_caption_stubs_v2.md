# Figure caption stubs (v2)

## Figure 1
Schematic of the reduced 2D electro-thermal model. (a) Computational geometry including the electrode, blood pool, myocardial wall, and bottom thermal buffer. The top boundary is modeled with convective cooling, while the side and lower boundaries are insulated. Wall thickness and insertion depth are varied parametrically. (b) Workflow of the electrical, thermal, and Arrhenius-damage subproblems and the resulting deterministic and probabilistic summary metrics.

## Figure 2
Representative spatial fields for a nominal case (wall thickness 4.0 mm, cooling coefficient 1500 W m^-2 K^-1, insertion depth 1.0 mm). (a) Final temperature fields for the three protocols. (b) Corresponding Arrhenius damage fields displayed as log10(Omega). Contours denote the lesion boundary defined by Omega = 1.

## Figure 3
Deterministic comparisons of the three radiofrequency protocols. (a) Depth fraction versus wall thickness at nominal contact and cooling. (b) Depth fraction versus insertion depth for a thin wall under nominal cooling. (c) Depth fraction versus cooling coefficient for a moderate-thickness wall under elevated contact. (d) Peak temperature versus insertion depth for a thin wall under nominal cooling.

## Figure 4
Verification of the numerical discretization. Relative error in lesion depth and peak temperature with respect to the finest solution is shown for (a) grid refinement and (b) time-step refinement. The selected operating discretization is highlighted.

## Figure 5
Transmural probability maps under uncertain contact and cooling conditions. Columns indicate nominal cooling levels and rows indicate the three protocol classes. Cell values are shown only for intermediate probabilities (0 < p < 1).

## Figure 6
Overheat probability maps under uncertain contact and cooling conditions. Columns indicate nominal cooling levels. Rows show the protocols in which overheating was observed within the scanned parameter space. Cell values are shown only for intermediate probabilities (0 < p < 1).

## Figure 7
Trade-off between median depth fraction and overheating probability for the scanned parameter combinations. Marker shape denotes protocol and marker color denotes wall thickness. Shaded regions indicate conditions favorable for deeper lesions with lower overheating risk.

## Figure 8
Trend-level literature-derived benchmark. (a) Agreement between reported lesion depth and simulated lesion depth for protocol-matched comparison points extracted from the literature. The dashed line indicates identity. (b) Protocol-level comparison of reported versus simulated mean lesion depth. Because experimental conditions are not geometry-matched to the reduced 2D model, this figure is interpreted as a trend-level benchmark rather than a strict pointwise validation.
