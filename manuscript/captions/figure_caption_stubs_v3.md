# Figure captions (v3)

## Figure 1. Reduced 2D electro-thermal model and workflow.
(a) Schematic of the 2D computational domain, including electrode, blood pool, myocardial wall, and bottom thermal buffer. The nominal insertion depth and wall thickness are indicated, together with insulated lateral/bottom boundaries and convective cooling at the top boundary. (b) Simulation workflow. Joule heating from the electrical subproblem is coupled to transient bioheat transfer and Arrhenius damage accumulation to derive lesion depth, depth fraction, transmurality, overheating area, and probability maps under uncertain contact and cooling.

## Figure 2. Representative temperature and damage fields for the three RF protocols.
Representative deterministic case with wall thickness 4.0 mm, cooling coefficient 1500 W m$^{-2}$ K$^{-1}$, and insertion depth 1.00 mm. (a) Temperature fields for Standard, HPSD, and vHPSD protocols, with the lesion boundary (defined by $\Omega=1$) overlaid. (b) Corresponding damage fields shown as $\log_{10}(\Omega)$, again with the lesion contour overlaid. The dashed horizontal line denotes the myocardial-wall / bottom-buffer interface.

## Figure 3. Deterministic protocol comparison.
(a) Depth fraction versus wall thickness at nominal cooling and contact. (b) Depth fraction versus insertion depth for a thin wall at nominal cooling. (c) Depth fraction versus cooling coefficient for a moderate-thickness wall at elevated contact. (d) Peak temperature versus insertion depth for a thin wall at nominal cooling, with the 100$^{\circ}$C reference threshold indicated.

## Figure 4. Numerical verification.
(a) Grid-refinement study showing the relative error in lesion depth and peak temperature with respect to the finest solution. (b) Time-step refinement study showing the corresponding relative errors. The selected grid and time-step settings used for the production simulations are highlighted.

## Figure 5. Transmural probability maps under uncertain contact and cooling.
Probability of achieving a transmural lesion as a function of nominal insertion depth and wall thickness under weak, nominal, and strong cooling. Rows correspond to Standard, HPSD, and vHPSD protocols. Only intermediate-probability cells are numerically annotated.

## Figure 6. Overheat probability maps under uncertain contact and cooling.
Probability of overheating as a function of nominal insertion depth and wall thickness under weak, nominal, and strong cooling. Rows correspond to HPSD and vHPSD protocols. The Standard protocol row is omitted because overheating was not observed under the scanned conditions.

## Figure 7. Trade-off between lesion depth and overheating risk.
Median depth fraction versus overheating probability for all scanned conditions. Marker shape denotes protocol and marker color denotes wall thickness. Panels correspond to weak, nominal, and strong cooling. The shaded region marks a favorable zone combining high depth fraction with low overheating risk.

## Figure 8. Literature-derived benchmark of lesion-depth trends.
(a) Agreement plot comparing reported lesion depth from the literature with the corresponding simulated lesion depth for protocol-matched reference cases. The dashed line indicates identity. (b) Protocol-level comparison of mean reported versus mean simulated lesion depth. This benchmark is intended as a trend-level comparison rather than a geometry-matched validation.

Median depth fraction under uncertain contact and cooling for Standard, HPSD, and vHPSD protocols across wall thicknesses and nominal insertion depths.

Transmural probability versus overheating probability for all scanned conditions. Marker shape denotes protocol and marker color denotes wall thickness.
