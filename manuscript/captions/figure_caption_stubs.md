# Figure caption stubs

## Figure 1
Two-dimensional reduced electro-thermal model used in this study. Panel (a) shows the simplified geometry and boundary conditions, including the electrode, blood pool, myocardial wall, and bottom thermal buffer. Panel (b) summarizes the computational workflow from the electrical subproblem to transient bioheat, Arrhenius damage, and deterministic/probabilistic summary outputs.

## Figure 2
Representative deterministic fields for the reference case (wall = 4.0 mm, h = 1500 W/m^2K, insertion = 1.00 mm). Panel (a) shows temperature distributions and the lesion boundary contour (Omega = 1) for Standard, HPSD, and vHPSD protocols. Panel (b) shows log10(Arrhenius Omega) maps for the same cases.

## Figure 3
Deterministic protocol comparisons showing the effects of wall thickness, insertion depth, and cooling on lesion depth fraction and peak temperature.

## Figure 4
Numerical verification results showing grid refinement and time-step refinement relative to the finest solution. The selected numerical settings used for the remaining simulations are highlighted.

Transmural probability maps under uncertain contact and cooling conditions for the Standard, HPSD, and vHPSD protocols. Only transition cells (0 < p < 1) are annotated.

## Figure 6
Overheat probability maps under uncertain contact and cooling conditions for the HPSD and vHPSD protocols. The Standard protocol is omitted because overheating was not observed in the scanned conditions.

## Figure 7
Trade-off summary between median depth fraction and overheating probability. Marker shape denotes protocol, and color denotes wall thickness.
