# Response to reviewers (v5)

We thank the reviewer for the constructive and technically precise assessment. We agree with the overall characterization that the manuscript had improved scientifically but still required a final round of technical correction before submission. In this revision we focused on manuscript/figure consistency, the explicit definition of the power-scaling and contact-surrogate rules, removal of revision-process wording from the manuscript body, clarification of the uncertainty-design description, and substantial expansion of the reference list.

## Comment 1. Figure 8 remained a placeholder in the submitted PDF.
**Response:** Agreed. Figure 8 has been replaced by a populated literature-derived benchmark figure. The figure no longer contains template text and the manuscript text, caption, and rebuttal now consistently describe it as a trend-level protocol benchmark rather than strict validation.

## Comment 2. Figure 3 caption did not match the actual figure.
**Response:** Agreed. The manuscript now includes the six-panel deterministic summary figure that matches the caption text. The caption and embedded artwork have been checked together in the final PDF.

## Comment 3. Supplementary Figure S3 did not match the text description.
**Response:** Agreed. Figure S3 has been replaced by the Wilson-score confidence-interval half-width distribution figure, which now matches both the main text and the supplementary caption.

## Comment 4. The power-scaling rule and contact surrogate remained insufficiently specified.
**Response:** Agreed. Section 2.4 now states the power-scaling and contact-surrogate rules explicitly. We define the regularized and depth-shifted source, the power scaling coefficient, the contact-gain factor applied to source amplitude, and the clipped cooling-reduction factor applied over the electrode footprint.

## Comment 5. The manuscript still contained revision-process language.
**Response:** Agreed. All phrases such as “after the stage16 revision”, “paper-level revised results”, and similar process-oriented wording were removed from the scientific manuscript and retained only in internal notes.

## Comment 6. Figures 5–6 could still be misread as low-sample probability maps.
**Response:** We clarified in Methods and captions that each nominal cell is based on 64 stratified samples and that probability uncertainty is summarized with 95% Wilson-score confidence intervals. Supplementary Figure S3 provides an explicit confidence-interval summary to support the stability of the displayed probability maps.

## Comment 7. The reference list remained too small.
**Response:** Agreed. The reference list was expanded from a small foundational set to a broader RF-cardiac-ablation modeling bibliography including classical thermal-injury references, irrigated-electrode modeling work, dynamic-contact modeling, HPSD/vHPSD experimental and clinical studies, contact-area work, and uncertainty-quantification background references.

Overall, we believe that the revised manuscript now addresses the major scientific and technical concerns raised in the previous review round.
